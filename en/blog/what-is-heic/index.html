What Is HEIC? The Format Your iPhone Uses for Every Photo

HEIC stands for High Efficiency Image Container. It is the default photo format used by Apple on iPhones, iPads, and Macs since iOS 11 was released in 2017. When you take a photo on a modern iPhone, the camera saves it as a .heic file unless you've changed the camera settings.

HEIC is built on the HEIF (High Efficiency Image File Format) standard, developed by the Moving Picture Experts Group (MPEG) — the same organisation behind MP3 and MP4. Photos inside a HEIC container are compressed using the HEVC (H.265) codec, the same technology used to compress 4K video efficiently.

The result: HEIC photos are approximately 50% smaller than equivalent JPG files while maintaining the same visual quality. That means twice as many photos on your iPhone without losing any detail.

💡 Quick fact: A typical iPhone photo in JPG is around 3–6 MB. The same photo in HEIC is roughly 1.5–3 MB — half the size with no visible quality difference.

Why Does Apple Use HEIC Instead of JPG?

Apple switched to HEIC as the default camera format in iOS 11 for one primary reason: storage efficiency. iPhone cameras have been shooting progressively higher resolution photos — from 12MP to 48MP on recent models — alongside 4K and 8K video. Without a more efficient format, photo libraries would consume device storage at an unsustainable rate.

HEIC solves this by cutting photo file sizes in half while preserving the same quality. On an iPhone with 128 GB of storage, switching from JPG to HEIC effectively doubles how many photos and videos you can store.

Additional reasons Apple chose HEIC include:

  • Live Photos support — HEIC can store multiple frames (the motion sequence) in a single file.
  • Depth maps — Portrait mode depth data can be embedded directly in the HEIC file.
  • HDR metadata — HEIC natively supports HDR images from iPhone cameras with Dolby Vision capture.
  • Transparency — Unlike JPG, HEIC supports alpha channels for images with transparent areas.
  • Lossless sequences — HEIC can store burst photos as a sequence in one file, rather than dozens of separate files.

💡 Smart transfer: Apple automatically converts HEIC to JPG when you AirDrop or email photos to non-Apple devices, or upload to certain apps. This is called "automatic conversion" and is enabled by default under Settings → Photos → Transfer to Mac or PC → Automatic.

How to Open HEIC Files on Windows 10 and 11

Windows 10 and Windows 11 do not support HEIC natively. If you transfer HEIC photos from your iPhone to a Windows PC, they may appear as broken thumbnails or fail to open in the Photos app.

You have three options:

Option 1: Install the HEIF Image Extensions (Free)

Microsoft offers a free extension in the Microsoft Store that adds HEIC support to Windows Photos, File Explorer thumbnails, and other apps.

1
Open the Microsoft Store on your Windows PC.
2
Search for "HEIF Image Extensions" and install the free version.
3
You may also need "HEVC Video Extensions" (~$1) for full support. The free HEIF extension alone handles most HEIC files.
4
After installing, restart File Explorer — your HEIC files will now display thumbnails and open in Windows Photos.

How to Stop Your iPhone from Saving Photos as HEIC

If you'd rather avoid HEIC compatibility issues entirely, you can switch your iPhone camera to save photos as JPG instead. The trade-off is larger file sizes and reduced storage efficiency.

1
Open Settings on your iPhone.
2
Scroll down and tap Camera.
3
Tap Formats.
4
Select Most Compatible instead of "High Efficiency". Your camera will now save photos as JPG.

💡 Better approach: Keep HEIC enabled for storage efficiency. Instead, go to Settings → Photos → Transfer to Mac or PC → Automatic. This keeps HEIC on your phone but automatically converts to JPG when you transfer photos via USB to a non-Apple computer.

HEIC vs HEIF: What's the Difference?

These two terms are closely related and often used interchangeably, but they refer to slightly different things:

  • HEIF (High Efficiency Image File Format) is the container format standard developed by MPEG. It defines how image data, metadata, thumbnails, and sequences can be stored in a single file.
  • HEIC (High Efficiency Image Container) is Apple's specific implementation of HEIF using the HEVC (H.265) video codec for compression.

Think of HEIF as the box, and HEIC as what Apple puts inside the box. The .heic extension is used on Apple devices. The .heif extension may appear on other platforms that implement the same standard. In practice, most tools treat them identically.

Frequently Asked Questions About HEIC

What is a HEIC file?
HEIC (High Efficiency Image Container) is Apple's default photo format on iPhone and iPad, introduced in iOS 11 (2017). It stores photos using HEVC (H.265) compression, producing files roughly 50% smaller than JPG at the same quality. It also supports transparency, HDR, depth maps, and multi-frame sequences like Live Photos.
Why can't I open HEIC files on Windows?
Windows does not include native HEIC support. To open HEIC on Windows, install the free HEIF Image Extensions from the Microsoft Store. Alternatively, convert your HEIC files to JPG using IMGVO's free online converter — no installation needed.

Is HEIC better than JPG?
Technically yes — HEIC produces files roughly 50% smaller than JPG at equivalent quality, and supports features JPG doesn't (transparency, HDR, multi-frame). The disadvantage is compatibility: HEIC isn't supported by most Windows apps, web browsers, or non-Apple devices without conversion.
How do I stop my iPhone from saving photos as HEIC?
Go to Settings → Camera → Formats and select Most Compatible. Your camera will save photos as JPG instead of HEIC. Note that JPG files are about twice the size, so you'll fill up storage faster.
What is the difference between HEIC and HEIF?
HEIF (High Efficiency Image File Format) is the international container standard developed by MPEG. HEIC is Apple's specific implementation using the HEVC codec. They are functionally the same for most purposes — the difference is just the name and file extension (.heic on Apple devices, .heif on others).
Can I upload HEIC to Instagram or Facebook?
Yes. Instagram, Facebook, and WhatsApp accept HEIC uploads and convert them automatically. However, for more control over quality and file size — especially for websites or email — it's better to convert HEIC to JPG yourself before uploading.
