Quick Answer: HEIC or JPG?

📱 HEIC for storage on Apple devices. JPG (JPEG) for sharing, web, and universal compatibility.

HEIC (also compared as HEIC vs JPEG) produces smaller files with better quality — typically 40–50% smaller than JPG at equal visual quality. However, HEIC has one critical limitation: it is not supported by any major web browser, Windows without extensions, or non-Apple platforms. JPG works everywhere without exception. For websites in 2026, neither is the best choice — use WebP instead, which beats both in file size and is supported by 97%+ of browsers globally.
